About Me

Civil Engineer & Researcher

I am currently pursuing my Master's in Civil Engineering at Jackson State University, where I work as a Graduate Research Assistant under Prof. Dr. Rocky Talchabhadel. My research focuses on hydrological impacts, climate change assessment, and multi-hazard vulnerability of coastal cities.

With a strong background in civil engineering and data science, I combine traditional engineering principles with modern computational methods to address complex environmental challenges. My work includes developing machine learning models for discharge prediction, conducting vulnerability assessments, and utilizing advanced geospatial tools for environmental analysis.

I am passionate about using technology and data-driven approaches to create sustainable solutions for infrastructure and environmental challenges, particularly in the context of climate change adaptation and disaster risk reduction.
